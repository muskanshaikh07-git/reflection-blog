**Learning about Failure wiki :-** 



In this unit, I have learned about the concepts of failure and how it doesn’t always have a negative outcome, but it's an important part of learning. Before learning this concept I used to think of failure as something that can be avoided but later this topic completely changed my perspective. Basically it shows me how failure can actually help us in understanding the situation better and improving future decisions.

The idea that stood out to me was how failures are often not caused by one single mistake but a series of small mistakes. This made me think differently about the problems, especially while working in a group. I can connect this with the real-life situation where things go wrong not because of one mistake but because of how different mistakes happened . Let’s take the example of the topic our team got which was on **“Mars Climate Orbiter”** where the mission failed due to a unit conversion mistake. At first I thought this was a very simple mistake but when I looked closely I realized that it's not just a one time or one person error. The failure happened because different teams were using different measurement systems and also the main issue was lack of communication and checking the system properly from the start . This shows that  failure builds over time due to small issues that are not mentioned or avoided. \\

Overall this topic  helped me to shift my thinking from blaming individuals to understanding the role of the entire team. By examining the example step by step I was able to understand the idea more clearly. One approach that worked for me was breaking down the situation and identifying where communication or understanding failed. This topic also changed my thinking about failure. Now I see it as a learning opportunity and not considering it as a  mistake so by this it can help me to improve the situation rather than just blaming individuals as a team. This perspective will help me in both academic and real life situations.
